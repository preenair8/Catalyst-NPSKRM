About the Program

Where curiosity becomes discovery.

Catalyst is a 12-week structured student research program at NPS Koramangala. Students move through every stage of real scientific inquiry — from forming a question to presenting findings.

Each student designs and conducts their own experiment, collects and analyses data, writes a formal research paper, and presents their work. No templates. No shortcuts. Real research.

── ◆ ──
Program Structure

12 Weeks. 5 Phases.

Each phase builds on the last — and ends with a concrete deliverable that students submit to the editorial board.

```
I

Weeks 1 – 3

Phase 1 → Question & Hypothesis

Deliverable Due Week 3

Students identify a genuine research question from any discipline, review background knowledge, and formulate a clear, testable hypothesis. The foundation of every great experiment is a great question.

Deliverable Written Research Question + Hypothesis Statement
II

Weeks 4 – 6

Phase 2 → Experiment & Data Collection

Deliverable Due Week 6

Students design and execute their experiment. Variables are identified, methods are documented precisely, and data is collected systematically under faculty guidance.

Deliverable Experiment Data & Lab Notes
III

Week 7

Phase 3 → Analysis

Deliverable Due Week 7

Raw data is transformed into insight. Students create graphs, identify patterns, interpret results, and draw evidence-based conclusions.

Deliverable Graphs + Written Analysis Document
IV

Weeks 8 – 10

Phase 4 → Research Paper

Deliverable Due Week 10

Students write a formally structured research paper following academic conventions — introduction, methodology, results, discussion, and references. Papers undergo review before final submission.

Deliverable Full Research Paper Draft (PDF or DOCX)
V

Weeks 11 – 12

Phase 5 → Presentation

Deliverable Due Week 11

Students present their research to an audience of peers, teachers, and parents. Communication, confidence, and clarity are the key outcomes of this final phase.

Deliverable Presentation Slides (PPT or PDF)
```
